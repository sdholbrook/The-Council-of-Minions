Forge Outcome

The Council of Minions

Hardened from a broad AI council concept into a focused Outlook-first system that reviews the full inbox, surfaces a narrow high-priority queue, and prepares delegation decisions for judgment-heavy email.

Outcome

  • Primary value is cleaner, faster delegation decisions from important email.
  • Missing important email remains a supporting constraint, not the north star.
  • The product is not a broad council or generic inbox copilot.

Core Shape

  • Review the full inbox.
  • Surface only a narrow high-priority queue plus lightweight secondary triage.
  • Keep Outlook as the main surface and the Minion Brief as a secondary view.

Locked Decisions

  • Important email means Doug-judgment email, especially relationship handling and priority/tradeoff decisions, plus legal/contract/finance exposure.
  • Default handling is delegation-first, with Doug approval over any outbound action.
  • Each flagged item must explain why it surfaced, the risk if ignored, the recommended action, personal-vs-delegable handling, and a draft reply when useful.
  • Delegation packages must include owner, owner rationale, stance or instruction, internal handoff draft, urgency, and external reply draft when needed.
  • Immediate interruption is reserved for same-day consequence, relationship damage risk, or worsening legal/contract/finance exposure.
  • If urgency and requested action are clear but owner confidence is only moderate, propose a best-guess owner with explicit confidence and rationale.
  • Doug-handles-this exceptions are role/relationship expectation, negotiation or concession, delegation-created risk, and tradeoff calls.

Rejected and Narrowed

  • Rejected the broad AI council framing as the primary product definition.
  • Rejected dashboard-first workflow in favor of Outlook-first adoption.
  • Rejected equal treatment across all email in favor of a narrow surfaced queue.
  • Rejected clean-but-risky under-flagging in favor of higher recall with stronger explanation.
  • Rejected interruption-by-default in favor of queue-first review with explicit interrupt triggers.

Weak Points That Survived

  • Owner selection can still be wrong when relationship context is thin.
  • Full-inbox review may create pressure to broaden Phase 1 beyond the sharp use case.
  • A high-recall system must keep explanations tight or the queue becomes unreviewable.
