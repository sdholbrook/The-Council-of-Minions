# Forged Idea

- Product: an Outlook-first delegation decision engine for judgment-heavy email, not a general AI council or generic inbox copilot.
- Input scope: review the full inbox.
- Surfaced scope: show a narrow high-priority queue plus lightweight triage for everything else.
- North star: turn important emails into cleaner, faster delegation decisions.
- Supporting constraint: do not miss important emails that need Doug's judgment.
- Important means: needs Doug's judgment, especially relationship handling or priority/tradeoff decisions, plus legal/contract/finance exposure.
- Default handling: prepare delegation first, but keep Doug approval over all outbound action.
- Each flagged item must include: why flagged, risk if ignored, recommended action, whether Doug must handle it or can delegate, and a draft reply when useful.
- Delegation package must include: suggested owner, why that owner, recommended stance/instruction, draft internal handoff, urgency, and draft external reply when needed.
- Interrupt only for: same-day consequence, relationship damage risk, or worsening legal/contract/finance exposure.
- Outlook is the primary surface; Minion Brief is secondary.
- Non-delegable exceptions: sender expects Doug personally, negotiation/concession/stance-setting, delegation itself creates risk, or a tradeoff call is required.
- Rejected shape: broad company-wide council, full equal-treatment inbox assistant, dashboard-first workflow, and under-flagging for cleanliness.
- Remaining risk: owner selection may still be wrong when confidence is only moderate; rationale and confidence must stay explicit.
