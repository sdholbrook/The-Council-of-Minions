- (note) Reviewed source patterns: Open Engine provides queue, private context, ledger, runner, receipts; Open Skills provides reusable operator-specific skills and runbooks; Open Brain provides governed memory, portable context bundles, and work operating model exports.
- (direction) User wants a controlled expansion beyond email to include task management and delegation of non-email items, with full human-agent traceability, persistent context/history, and a clear ontology/knowledge graph informed by Open Engine and Open Brain concepts.
- (lock) The Council of Minions should become work-item-first, with email as one source among others rather than the primary product boundary.
- (direction) Provisional choice: work item is the canonical object, provided it can be typed and attributed through the ontology and knowledge graph.
- (lock) Use a universal work-item shell with a type field plus graph links to people, projects, decisions, commitments, artifacts, and sources rather than multiple first-class execution objects.
- (lock) Phase 1 starter work-item types: decision, delegation, follow_up, request, risk, artifact_task, and meeting_action.
- (lock) Use an explicit two-step model: source records enter first, the system proposes work items from them, and work items link back to source records and graph entities.
- (lock) Use a mixed creation model: auto-create low-risk work items like follow_up and meeting_action at high confidence, require human approval for decision, delegation, and risk items, and always preserve source link plus rationale.
- (lock) Defer the canonical data-store choice until requirements are defined; Dataverse, Planner/To Do, Cosmos DB, Horizon-style stores, and Fabric remain architecture options rather than settled dependencies.
- (lock) Top canonical trace-layer requirements are: one canonical typed work-item record, graph links to related entities, and an append-only audit trail of human and agent actions.
- (lock) Source-to-work-item provenance with rationale is a top-tier requirement alongside canonical work item, graph links, and append-only audit.
- (lock) Phase 1 graph should be lightly operational, with enough structure to support routing and context now while preserving a path to a more strongly operational graph later.
